# How Does the Model Count in French?

## 1. Executive Summary
We tested whether modern LLMs represent French counting patterns, especially the irregular `70-99` range (`soixante-dix`, `quatre-vingt-dix` family), in a measurable and mappable way.

Key finding: for numeral transduction tasks, models were near-perfect and did **not** degrade on `70-99`; the strongest band effect appeared in a controlled *pattern classification* probe, where irregular-range items were easier than regular-range items under our label schema.

Practical implication: current strong LLMs can reliably map French numeral words and digits in low-temperature settings, but probe design strongly affects conclusions about “representation difficulty.”

## 2. Goal
### Hypothesis
Large language models represent and process French counting in a way that can be mapped and analyzed, despite its counterintuitive structure.

### Why this matters
French numeric forms are compositionally transparent but atypical. Reliable handling affects multilingual education, translation, and safety-critical numeric communication.

### Problem solved
We provide a reproducible probe pipeline to quantify where models succeed/fail in French counting and how those failures distribute by number band.

## 5. Result Analysis
### Key Findings
1. `digit_to_word` and `word_to_digit` performance is extremely high across bands; no strong irregular-band penalty in this setup.
2. `word_to_digit` is ceiling-level (100%) in all complete model/condition runs.
3. `pattern_classification` shows large, significant band differences (FDR-corrected p < 0.001) favoring irregular-band items.
4. For MGSM-FR, reasoning prompt improved `gpt-4.1` from 48.8% to 57.5%.

### Hypothesis Testing
- The broad hypothesis (“representation can be mapped and analyzed”) is supported.
- The specific expectation of degraded `70-99` transduction is **not** supported in this deterministic setup.

Selected statistical results:
- `gpt-4.1 direct digit_to_word`: risk diff = +0.0059, p = 0.6737 (ns)
- `gpt-4.1-mini direct digit_to_word`: risk diff = -0.0216, p = 0.3702 (ns)
- `pattern_classification` effects significant after FDR for all complete runs

Logistic model (`n=1775`) highlights:
- Irregular-band coefficient positive and significant (p ≈ 1.19e-9)
- Condition effect (reasoned vs direct) not significant in pooled core probes (p ≈ 0.83)

### Error Analysis
Representative transduction failure:
- `71`: gold `soixante et onze`, prediction `soixante-et-onze` (orthographic variant)

Most observed “errors” are formatting/variant issues rather than numeric misunderstanding.

### Limitations
- Final block (`gpt-4.1-mini` reasoned) was partial due runtime constraints.
- Pattern-classification probe may privilege irregular forms by label design.
- No token-level logprob/mechanistic activation analysis in this run.

## 6. Conclusions
The study successfully maps French counting behavior for strong API LLMs with reproducible probes and statistics. In this configuration, models do not show a clear irregular-range weakness for direct numeral transduction; they are nearly perfect. The strongest observable structure appears in classification-style probes and in broader math reasoning (MGSM), where prompting has larger impact than numeral transduction itself.

Confidence is moderate-high for the completed conditions and moderate overall due one incomplete model-condition block.
