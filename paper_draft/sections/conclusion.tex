\section{Conclusion}
\label{sec:conclusion}
We presented \frcountmap, a reproducible probe framework for French numeral representation. Across 1,935 analyzed outputs, \digitword and \worddigit are near-ceiling to ceiling, with no significant irregular-band penalty for completed transduction conditions. Significant band effects emerge mainly in \patterncls, and reasoning prompts matter more for \mgsmfr than for core numeral transduction.

The key takeaway is that modern LLMs can reliably map French numeral forms in deterministic settings, but claims about representation difficulty depend strongly on probe design.

Next steps are straightforward: complete the missing \gptfouronemini reasoned block, expand normalization to explicitly include orthographic reform variants, add mechanistic probes on open models, and test out-of-distribution variants such as Belgian/Swiss forms (e.g., \textit{septante}, \textit{nonante}).
